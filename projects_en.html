MASTER'S THESIS:

Artificial Intelligence and Computer Vision for Automated Recognition of Assembly Steps in the Context of Assistance Systems

(...currently in progress)

Task Description

The use of Worker Assistance Systems (WAS) in manual assembly has significantly increased over the past few years. Information that was previously implicit or explicit knowledge among employees or rudimentary accessible via a MES is now directly provided to employees through WAS. WAS are still very complex in terms of authorizing content and implementing context sensitivity. For this reason, an automated recognition of activities in the assembly area is to be conceived, implemented, and validated using AI methods. This involves using Computer Vision, Human Activity Recognition (HAR), and Speech recognition to identify movements, activities, etc. These results should then be transferred via an interface to an assistance system and other systems, to create assistance steps or automatically check the correctness of assistance steps.

BACHELOR'S THESIS:

Development of a Mobile Collaborative Robot System for Foaming Processes in the Automotive Industry

Task Description

To increase flexibility and support the local staff, it is planned to automate the foaming process for the production of nozzles. This will be done with a robotic system that replaces the manual placement of the mixing head on the tool opening. What's special about this robotic system is that it should be able to independently recognize the positions of the tools and their openings, instead of driving to predefined positions. This increases flexibility since the arrangement and number of foaming stations and tools often change in the worker halls. The robotic system must function in new environments without manual reprogramming and be able to integrate into existing facilities. It should be able to determine two positions for each tool: the location of the tool and the exact position of the opening. Since the tools vary in size and shape, the positions of the openings vary in X, Y, and Z directions. The robotic system must also function in real industrial environments.

Prototyp

With the successful completion of the Bachelor's thesis, a complete prototype was realized, a mobile manipulator consisting of two subsystems. This prototype was developed independently and is characterized by a careful conception and design, aimed at meeting specific requirements. The development process included designing in various domains such as mechanics, electronics, and computer science. Following these domain-specific designs, system integration took place, where the individual domains were combined into a functioning overall system. A crucial step was the validation and verification of the prototype, to check whether all requirements were met and where further work might be needed. The development, design, and construction of the prototype were done in independent work.

Interface diagram

The interface diagram illustrates the composition and interactions between the two subsystems of the overall system. Subsystem 1 includes a chassis, equipped with a variety of sensors that enable autonomous driving. A key sensor is the lane guidance sensor, which detects specially marked lanes marked by fluorescent bands on the ground. Additionally, an RFID device is included, which captures the position of the tools and allows the vehicle to position itself accordingly. The movement is powered by two motors, controlled via H-bridges. The core of this subsystem is a Controllino PLC control unit, programmed in C/C++. Subsystem 2 comprises a collaborative robot, the UR3, equipped with necessary sensors like a camera and a distance sensor. These sensors enable the robot to precisely locate and approach the holes of the tools to be filled. The interface diagram shows how these two subsystems interact to ensure a seamless and efficient operation. It illustrates the data flows and control commands between the chassis and the robot, with a special focus on the integration of the different sensor technologies and control units.
